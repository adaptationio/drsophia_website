Splice's workaround was to use HubSpot forms as the automation trigger, with each form firing a fixed 3-email sequence with 2-day delays. No branching, no booking-awareness, no funnel-stage updates. Just: form submitted → email 1 → wait 2d → email 2 → wait 2d → email 3.

Michel acknowledges this in his note: "the Hubspot subscription won't allow us to track who has booked and who hasn't (automated workflows) and therefore assumes all leads have yet to book." A patient who books with Dr Dia on day 1 still gets the day-3 nurture email asking them to book. That's a UX risk and Michel flags it explicitly.
