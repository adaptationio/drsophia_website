Yogesh's Due Diligence Questions (Received Mar 27)

Julia's signed responses were sent Mar 30 as PDF. Below are the questions AND the exact answers that were sent. Tshikululu should have reviewed these already.

Q1. Commercial Model — What business model(s) do you currently use?

Dr Sophia AI is offered as Software as a Solution (SaaS) licence. Standard commercial model is Partnered Enterprise enabling: National Set Up (regulator-specific), High Volume Low Cost per-transaction delivery, Sovereign Capability (train + qualify local teams), Regional Export to regulatory-alike nations. For this initiative, open to case-by-case pricing — working to deliver cost coverage whilst maximising social impact for SA and creating SA-led export opportunity.

Q2. Commercial Model — Pricing sustainability, volumes, scalability?

Partnered Enterprise SaaS is fastest to deploy and lowest CAPEX. JV was investigated but cost-prohibitive. Key: Fixed price band ceiling on token costs. Future SLM evolution will reduce costs >5X. AWS architecture = unlimited global scale. "We will anchor cost to what the programme can sustain." Model can flex across insurer, NGO, and clinic settings without one-size-fits-all contract.

Q3. Regulatory — SAHPRA SaMD submissions?

Current deployments operate on 100% clinician-in-the-loop. Sophia functions as CDSS. In Australia, completed registration of first AI clinician under TGA SaMD Class I. SAHPRA published AI/ML device guidance Aug 2025 (MD08-2025/2026) using IMDRF-aligned framework (same as FDA). Class A low-risk CDSS pathway is the likely immediate compliance route. Foresee full short-term compliance equivalent to TGA and FDA per 2026 guidance.

Q4. Regulatory — In-country precedent?

No South African precedent yet — this would be first African deployment. Have regulatory precedent in Australia (TGA SaMD Class I). See Tshikululu's local governance expertise as key asset in navigating SAHPRA. SA precedent will also enable export within the region.

Q5. Regulatory — Support for local legal/data-protection?

Provide documentation support, regulatory mapping, technical evidence. Already completed SAHPRA compliance analysis (shared). Would work with local legal and regulatory partner — where Tshikululu adds direct value. Architecture supports data residency; POPIA compliance scoped as part of pilot design, plus encryption and immutable audit logs.

Q6. Liability — How is liability managed? KEY ANSWER

This is the answer Dwayne specifically modified. Two scenarios depending on SAHPRA's position: (1) If regulator allows AI CDSS to recommend an approved prescription (like US FDA), the tool can issue diagnostic and prescriptive recommendations within guidelines. (2) If regulator is risk-averse, prescribing function is disabled — Glass-Box Class I CDSS showing differential diagnosis, reasoning, certainty rating, and treatment plan for clinician review. "In both outcomes: the AI tool informs, clinician decides, clinician is accountable. Liability follows clinical authority."

Q7. Liability — Indemnity structures?

Dr Sophia AI carries liability for accuracy, availability, and integrity of software outputs. Operator carries liability for ensuring outputs reviewed by qualified clinician. "We do not indemnify clinical decisions — those sit with the practitioner." Product liability cover maintained for the technology. SA deployment indemnity localised to HPCSA obligations + SA law, worked through with Tshikululu.

Q8. Clinical — Practitioner oversight + cost?

Yes — 100% human in the loop. Every deployment operates under authority of registered practitioner. On cost: practitioner is NOT an add-on cost — a qualified clinician is already present in every deployment context. "The model is not 'Sophia + extra clinical overhead' — it is 'existing clinician, significantly enhanced.'"

Q9. Market — Insurer/medical scheme experience?

Haven't yet worked with large insurers directly — emerging channel. Clear view of value in 4 areas: (1) Actuarial/claims assessment, (2) Clinical support tool for insurer-contracted providers, (3) Prior authorisation support, (4) Wellness/managed care programmes (diabetes, hypertension, HIV).

Q10. Market — Breakeven in high-volume, low-margin?

Token-based cost structure. 30-min consultation = USD $0.17 direct cost. Per-unit economics improve with volume. Three levers: volume, value attribution, social impact. Recommend anchoring on cost-per-outcome not cost-per-transaction (cost per avoided adverse event, per chronic condition managed). Middle East reference: 90% cost reduction to patient, Sophia commercialised off remaining 10%, 51% operating profit from Year 5.

This is significant — CIPLA is one of the world's largest generic pharma companies (revenue ~$2.5B), with extensive African operations including manufacturing in South Africa. A CIPLA nursing programme pilot would provide:

Data Privacy & Sovereign Data Position Statement

Also included in the signed Q&A document (page 5). Key statements:

  • "Patient data stays where patients are" — in-country data residency by default
  • Cloud infrastructure with in-country or in-region hosting partners
  • Aligns with POPIA (SA), Privacy Act (AU), GDPR (EU), HIPAA (US) — designed to meet most stringent standard
  • Formal data processing agreement with every deployment partner
  • "We do not use patient data to train or improve our models without explicit, informed consent"
  • For SA: Tshikululu (or clinical partner) = responsible party under POPIA; Dr Sophia AI = data operator
  • "Just as clinical authority stays with the clinician, data sovereignty stays with the country. These are not policy positions — they are how Sophia is architected."
