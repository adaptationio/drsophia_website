Q1 · Commercial model

SaaS Partnered Enterprise framework: National Set Up → High-Volume Low-Cost per-transaction → Sovereign Capability → Regional Export. Case-by-case pricing for this initiative to maximise social impact + SA-led regional export.

Q2 · Pricing sustainability

Lowest CAPEX option — JV was cost-prohibitive. Fixed ceiling on token costs. AWS global scale; co-design infrastructure with partner nation. "We will anchor cost to what the programme can sustain."

Q3 · SAHPRA pathway

100% clinician-in-the-loop; CDSS only. TGA SaMD Class I done in AU. SAHPRA MD08-2025/2026 is IMDRF-aligned (same regulatory family as FDA). Class A low-risk CDSS pathway likely.

Q4 · In-country precedent

None in SA yet (would be first African deployment). AU precedent + Tshikululu's local governance = the route. SA precedent then enables regional export.

Q5 · Local legal / data-protection support

Documentation, regulatory mapping, technical evidence. Already did SAHPRA compliance analysis. Work with local legal partner. Architecture supports data residency; POPIA scoped into pilot; encryption + immutable audit logs.

Q6 · Liability (Dwayne-edited)

Two scenarios:
(a) If SAHPRA follows FDA, the tool can recommend a single approved prescription.
(b) If risk-averse, prescribing disabled — Glass-Box Class I CDSS showing differential + reasoning + certainty for clinician to act on.
"AI informs, clinician decides, clinician accountable. Liability follows clinical authority."

Q7 · Indemnity

Dr Sophia AI liable for software output accuracy, availability, integrity. Operator liable for clinician review. "We do not indemnify clinical decisions." Product liability cover held. SA indemnity localised to HPCSA + SA law.

Q8 · Clinical oversight + its cost

100% human-in-loop. The practitioner is not an add-on cost — already present in every deployment. "Existing clinician, significantly enhanced."

Q9 · Insurer / medical-scheme experience

None direct yet. Four value areas:
(1) actuarial / claims,
(2) clinical support for insurer-contracted providers,
(3) prior-authorisation support,
(4) wellness / managed care (diabetes, hypertension, HIV).

Q10 · Break-even / high-volume low-margin

Token-based. USD $0.17 per 30-min consult. Three levers: volume, value attribution, social impact. Anchor on cost-per-outcome not cost-per-transaction. Middle-East reference: 90% patient-cost reduction, Sophia off 10%, 51% operating profit by Year 5.

Data Privacy & Sovereign Data — Position Statement

"Patient data stays where patients are." In-country residency by default. Aligns POPIA + AU Privacy Act + GDPR + HIPAA — designed to meet most stringent standard. "We do not use patient data to train or improve our models without explicit, informed consent." Tshikululu (or clinical partner) = responsible party under POPIA; Dr Sophia AI = data operator.

"Just as clinical authority stays with the clinician, data sovereignty stays with the country. These are not policy positions — they are how Sophia is architected."
