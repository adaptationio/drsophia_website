About this document

This white paper (Version 4.2, December 2025, prepared for Nature Digital Medicine) documents the architecture, benchmark evaluation methodology, and safety validation results for Dr Sophia AI's multi-provider Byzantine consensus platform.

What's inside

  • System architecture — 42-agent distribution across 4 providers, Byzantine formula (n ≥ 3f+1), consensus thresholds, team structure (Triage / Research / Diagnosis / Treatment / Safety / Validation)
  • ANY-1-VETO asymmetric safety pattern — design rationale, trigger conditions, trade-offs, observed veto rates
  • Three-tier hybrid safety architecture — 41,580-record deterministic medical database, AI-augmented verification, conservative fallback
  • Deterministic tool integration — 15 FHIR tools, 9 clinical calculators, MedAgentBench validation
  • Agent-Based Reinforcement Learning (ABRL / Orchestration Layer Optimization)
  • Benchmark methodology — stratified random sampling, ground-truth handling, clinical controversy adjudication, dataset-error treatment, exact binomial 95% confidence intervals
  • Error correlation analysis across same-model vs multi-provider configurations
  • Cost · latency · consensus threshold sensitivity analysis
  • Full limitations section and prospective clinical validation roadmap

Key references

  • MedQA-USMLE: Jin et al. (2021), Appl. Sci. 11, 6421
  • MedAgentBench: Jiang et al. (2025), NEJM AI 2(9), AIdbp2500144
  • Byzantine generals problem: Lamport, Shostak & Pease (1982), ACM TOPLAS 4(3)
  • GPT-4 medical baseline: Nori et al. (2023), arXiv:2303.13375
  • Clinical LLMs: Singhal et al. (2023), Nature 620, 172–180
Important notes. This document reports benchmark performance against public medical question-answering datasets and synthetic safety scenarios generated from authoritative guidelines. It is not a claim of ARTG registration or TGA approval; Dr Sophia AI operates under the TGA CDSS Exemption pathway (Rule 2.15, Schedule 4, Therapeutic Goods (Medical Devices) Regulations 2002). Prospective clinical validation with real patient data is necessary before deployment recommendations can be made with confidence. The multi-provider platform is a clinical decision support system and does not replace professional clinical judgement by AHPRA-registered practitioners.
